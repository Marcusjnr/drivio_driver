My read

B (Editorial Serif + Sans) is my recommendation. The serif gives Drivio editorial gravitas — it's the type system you'd expect from a travel magazine or a thoughtful brand, which is exactly what "warm premium people's brand" needs to differentiate from generic tech sans. Coastal Pulse is a saturated palette; a serif anchor balances that energy with restraint. The serif route is also rare in African mobility brands — real differentiation. Fraunces is variable and can dial warmth (the SOFT axis) so we control how editorial vs how friendly it reads. Inter handles UI cleanly. A is safer but blends in. C is fresh but reads techier than the personality we picked.

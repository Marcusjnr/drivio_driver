Drivio
Coral disc with Marcellus "D" reversed in ivory. Reads as a wax seal — heritage, premium, deliberate. The classical D inside the coral feels signed and intentional. Best for: app icon clarity, embroidery (the D reads even at thread scale), business cards. Travels confidently across all three apps. Risk: a monogram inside a disc is a familiar move; the craft of the D specifically has to be excellent to avoid feeling generic.

Drivio
The dot from Z stays a dot — but it becomes the entire brand mark. A coral dot on charcoal-teal background as the app icon. The period at the end of "Drivio." is the same dot, scaled down. Single ownable shape. The most restrained, most confident, most pan-African-modernist of the three. Risk: minimalism only works if the dot's placement and supporting system are rigorous; without discipline it reads as "nothing." Strongest with brand mythology behind it (the dot = the destination = arrival).

My read

3 (The Dot, Period) is the boldest and most confident choice — it commits hardest to the Z direction and gives Drivio a single ownable shape. Risk is also highest because minimalism needs unwavering discipline. 2 (The Pin) is the most functionally honest — the brand mark and the product's pickup pin are the same shape, which is unusually beautiful integration. 1 (The Sealed D) is the safest, most directly heritage-coded — works everywhere, reads premium, but the move is familiar.

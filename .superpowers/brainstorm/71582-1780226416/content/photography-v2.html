Photography direction — with actual photographs

Real photos this time (sourced from Unsplash). The final brand pass would commission or license from specific African photographers — but for committing a direction, these show the mood. Click your pick.

A · Hybrid
Recommended

Editorial portrait + cinematic urbanscape

"Portraits for hero. Cityscape for context."

Two-mode system. Close, lit, dignified portraits of drivers and passengers hold the people story. Wide cinematic city shots — golden hour, blue hour, night — hold the place story. Together they cover person and city without either dominating.

Best for: brand campaigns, splash, OOH, social. Scales because the modes are clearly distinct (portrait vs landscape) — the brand team always knows which mode they're shooting.

References: Cash App brand campaigns · Aesop · MR PORTER travel features · Apple TV+ premium documentaries

B · Editorial

Editorial portrait, art-directed

"All people, all the time."

Every brand image is a portrait. Lit. Composed. Magazine-grade. Subjects looking at camera or in considered moments. Maximum human warmth and premium polish; the city becomes abstract context behind the face.

Risk: without urban context, brand can feel slightly disembodied from the markets it operates in. Best paired with strong copy that names the place ("Drivers from Lagos, Accra, Nairobi").

References: Aesop campaigns · NYT magazine portraits · Off-White campaigns · Vogue Africa

C · Candid

Candid documentary

"Real moments. Naturally lit. Slightly imperfect."

Drivers between rides, passengers in transit, mid-action shots. Natural light only, no studio styling, slight motion blur welcome. Reads truthful, lived-in, genuinely Lagos / Accra / Nairobi.

Risk: harder to scale (every shoot needs a real context, not repeatable in studio). Can read less premium than the warm editorial direction we picked at the strategy level. Strong if Drivio wants to feel grassroots-and-real.

References: Airbnb "Belong Anywhere" · Lyft early campaigns · Patagonia environmental work · Stephen Tayo street photography

D · Cinematic

Cinematic urbanscape, people in context

"The city, atmospheric. People in scale."

Wide cinematic shots of African cityscapes at blue hour, golden hour, neon-lit night. People are present but small in frame — silhouettes, scale-givers, not subjects. Drama from light.

Risk: the People's Brand center of gravity (human warmth) gets sublimated to atmosphere. Place becomes the character; person becomes context. Strong if you want Drivio to feel like an Apple TV+ travel doc.

References: Apple TV+ premium docs · Spike Lee opening sequences · Sebastião Salgado · Christopher Doyle for Wong Kar-wai
